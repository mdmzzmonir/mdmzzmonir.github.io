01 · About

Fieldwork first, models second

I'm a Master's candidate in Economics at Khulna University, working at the intersection of energy poverty, climate vulnerability, and household welfare in Bangladesh's southwestern coastal belt — one of the regions most exposed to salinity intrusion, cyclones, and unreliable power access.

My path here started with a BSS thesis on technology adoption in paddy farming in Gaibandha, and has since taken me through three years of direct household-level fieldwork: salinity-free drinking water, water governance, multidimensional poverty, and organic food consumption, before settling on energy poverty as the throughline connecting climate risk to everyday household decisions.

I care about econometrics that stays honest to the survey data behind it — which is why I still enumerate my own surveys rather than working only from secondary datasets.
